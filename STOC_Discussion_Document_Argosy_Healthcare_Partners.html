Section 02

Firm Overview

Argosy Healthcare Partners is the healthcare strategy of Argosy Capital, a Wayne, PA investment firm founded in 1990. AHP invests exclusively in lower middle market healthcare, partnering with founder-owned companies through control transactions with significant rollover.

Investment Strategy

Control investments in US-based healthcare services and products businesses, executed through recapitalizations that keep founders invested via significant equity rollover. Equity checks run up to $20M or larger with co-investment.

Target Profile

Founder-owned healthcare companies with $1M to $3M of EBITDA and $5M to $30M enterprise value. Recent platforms span healthcare staffing, medical device contract manufacturing, life sciences tools, and self-insured services.

Operating Philosophy

The firm describes itself as thesis driven, relationship focused, and a conservative business builder, dedicated exclusively to healthcare and the lower middle market. A dedicated portfolio operations function, led at the partner level, supports value creation across platforms.

Value Creation Approach

AHP professionalizes founder-built companies by installing experienced CEOs and boards, reinvesting in the business, and pursuing growth. Its first realization, Specialty Care Management, exited in June 2025 after roughly a three-year hold with 3x revenue growth.

Section 03

Portfolio Activity and Investment Trends

Observations drawn from the firm's portfolio, fund activity, and public announcements.

01
Acquisition pace accelerated into a newly closed fund
Why this matters
AHP added three platforms in 2024 and two more in 2025 (O.R. Staffing in August, IS Benefits in October), then held the final close of Fund I in October 2025. Deployment cadence is rising as fresh capital becomes available.
02
Healthcare staffing is the dominant platform theme
Why this matters
Three of six active platforms are staffing businesses: Connect Life Sciences, Nicklas Medical Staffing, and O.R. Staffing Solutions, spanning life sciences, lab, and operating room staffing. This concentration points to a repeatable thesis.
03
Recapitalization model anchored in founder rollover
Why this matters
Each platform is structured as a recapitalization and partnership with founder-owned businesses, taking majority control with significant rollover. Acquired companies often arrive without institutional finance and reporting standards.
04
Portfolio leadership professionalized at scale in 2025
Why this matters
AHP installed three new CEOs and four new board members across the portfolio in 2025, and named Megan Driscoll Executive Chairman of Connect Life Sciences in June 2026. New leadership typically resets reporting and operating expectations.
05
First realization validated the value creation model
Why this matters
Specialty Care Management exited in June 2025 to a PE-backed strategic buyer with 3x revenue growth over the hold. With Fund I deployed across seven platforms, the portfolio is moving from acquisition into value creation and eventual exit.
06
Footprint weighted toward Florida
Why this matters
Three platforms are headquartered in Florida (Command Medical in Ormond Beach, Connect Life Sciences in Orlando, Nicklas in Jacksonville), with others in New Hampshire, Illinois, New Jersey, and California. Clustering can support regional sourcing and shared standards.

Section 05

Portfolio Signals and STOC Relevance

Specific, evidence-based signals that connect portfolio observations to STOC capabilities. Intended to inform conversation, not to sell.

01
A freshly closed Fund I and rising deployment cadence increase the need for consistent proprietary pipeline.
Why it matters
With Fund I closed in October 2025 and a small investment team, sourcing capacity becomes a practical constraint on deal throughput for both new platforms and add-ons.
Potential STOC Alignment  ·  CDS
Proprietary sourcing, market mapping, and founder outreach embedded alongside the deal team, accountable to the pipeline.
02
Three of six platforms are healthcare staffing businesses across distinct clinical and scientific niches.
Why it matters
Staffing platforms scale through add-on acquisition and specialty or geographic expansion, which depends on a steady flow of qualified, non-competitive targets.
Potential STOC Alignment  ·  CDS
Add-on sourcing and market mapping within life sciences, lab, and clinical staffing verticals.
03
A lean deal team executes diligence on founder-owned targets that often carry unaudited or cash-basis financials.
Why it matters
Ryan Noon and Benjamin Henwood anchor concurrent transactions; founder financials usually require normalization before they can be underwritten with confidence.
Potential STOC Alignment  ·  TAS
Quality of Earnings, EBITDA normalization, and net working capital analysis delivered partner-led and on deal timelines.
04
New CEOs and boards were installed across the portfolio in 2025 and 2026.
Why it matters
Newly installed leaders standardize KPIs and reporting in their first year, yet founder-built reporting is often inconsistent across a growing platform base.
Potential STOC Alignment  ·  FDI
KPI reporting, board pack design, and dashboard development that give new operators a consistent performance view.
05
The first exit is complete and the earliest platforms are maturing toward realization.
Why it matters
After the Specialty Care Management exit, attention shifts to value creation and exit readiness; buyer-ready data and historical KPI continuity support valuation.
Potential STOC Alignment  ·  FDI  ·  TAS
Exit readiness dashboards, data room preparation, and sell-side financial readiness as holds mature.
06
Staffing platforms run recruiter-driven, high-volume commercial models.
Why it matters
Revenue performance hinges on pipeline, fill rates, and recruiter productivity, which depend on disciplined CRM and commercial reporting to scale.
Potential STOC Alignment  ·  GES
CRM build out, revenue operations, and commercial KPI reporting across the staffing platforms.
